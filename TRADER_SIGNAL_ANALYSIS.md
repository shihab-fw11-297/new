# XAUUSD Signal Analysis Guide

This guide explains signal generation from a trader's point of view only.

## Core Idea

A signal is not created because price is moving up or down.

A signal is created only when price shows a complete trade idea:

1. Liquidity exists.
2. Liquidity is attacked or swept.
3. Price reacts with displacement.
4. Structure supports the direction.
5. Price returns to a valid entry area.
6. A closed candle confirms entry.
7. Stop loss, target, and risk-reward are acceptable.

If any major part is missing, the idea remains pending, expires, or is rejected.

## Signal Lifecycle

### 1. Market Observation

The chart is first read for market behavior:

- Is price trending, ranging, or choppy?
- Is price near a major high or low?
- Is price in premium, discount, or equilibrium?
- Is liquidity resting above or below price?
- Is momentum expanding or fading?
- Is the current candle showing buyers or sellers in control?

At this stage there is no trade signal.

This stage only builds market context.

## 2. Liquidity Level Detection

Liquidity levels are areas where traders likely have stops, pending orders, or breakout orders.

### Buy-Side Liquidity

Buy-side liquidity is usually above price.

Common locations:

- Previous swing high
- Equal highs
- Session high
- Range high
- Previous day high
- Obvious resistance

Trader meaning:

Price moving above this area may be hunting buy stops or breakout buyers.

Possible use:

- A sweep above buy-side liquidity can create a bearish reversal idea.
- A clean break and hold above it can support bullish continuation.

### Sell-Side Liquidity

Sell-side liquidity is usually below price.

Common locations:

- Previous swing low
- Equal lows
- Session low
- Range low
- Previous day low
- Obvious support

Trader meaning:

Price moving below this area may be hunting sell stops or breakout sellers.

Possible use:

- A sweep below sell-side liquidity can create a bullish reversal idea.
- A clean break and hold below it can support bearish continuation.

## 3. Swing Structure

Swing structure defines the market's visible turning points.

### Swing High

A swing high is a price peak where price pushed up and then turned lower.

Trader meaning:

- Resistance reference
- Buy-side liquidity area
- Possible target for bullish trades
- Possible reversal area if swept

### Swing Low

A swing low is a price valley where price pushed down and then turned higher.

Trader meaning:

- Support reference
- Sell-side liquidity area
- Possible target for bearish trades
- Possible reversal area if swept

## 4. Liquidity Sweep

A sweep happens when price trades beyond a liquidity level and then rejects that area.

### Bullish Sweep

A bullish sweep forms when price takes sell-side liquidity below a low, then rejects lower prices.

Typical behavior:

- Price breaks below a prior low.
- Sellers enter late.
- Stops below the low are triggered.
- Price closes back higher.
- Buyers begin absorbing sell pressure.

Trader interpretation:

This can be the beginning of a long setup.

Not enough by itself:

A sweep alone is not a buy signal. It only says liquidity was taken.

### Bearish Sweep

A bearish sweep forms when price takes buy-side liquidity above a high, then rejects higher prices.

Typical behavior:

- Price breaks above a prior high.
- Buyers enter late.
- Stops above the high are triggered.
- Price closes back lower.
- Sellers begin absorbing buy pressure.

Trader interpretation:

This can be the beginning of a short setup.

Not enough by itself:

A sweep alone is not a sell signal. It only says liquidity was taken.

## 5. Displacement

Displacement is a strong directional candle or candle sequence after liquidity is taken.

### Bullish Displacement

Bullish displacement means buyers entered with force.

Signs:

- Strong bullish body
- Close near candle high
- Breaks minor resistance
- Moves away from swept low
- Little hesitation after sweep

Trader meaning:

The sweep may have trapped sellers, and buyers may now control short-term price.

### Bearish Displacement

Bearish displacement means sellers entered with force.

Signs:

- Strong bearish body
- Close near candle low
- Breaks minor support
- Moves away from swept high
- Little hesitation after sweep

Trader meaning:

The sweep may have trapped buyers, and sellers may now control short-term price.

## 6. Market Structure Shift

A market structure shift confirms that price is no longer behaving the same way.

### Bullish Structure Shift

Bullish shift signs:

- Price sweeps a low.
- Price displaces upward.
- Price breaks a minor swing high.
- Pullback holds above the swept low.

Trader meaning:

Short-term control may have shifted from sellers to buyers.

### Bearish Structure Shift

Bearish shift signs:

- Price sweeps a high.
- Price displaces downward.
- Price breaks a minor swing low.
- Pullback holds below the swept high.

Trader meaning:

Short-term control may have shifted from buyers to sellers.

## 7. Entry Zone

An entry zone is the area where price should return after displacement.

It is not a signal by itself.

Common entry zones:

- Sweep retest zone
- Displacement midpoint
- Fair value gap area
- Order-block-like candle area
- Premium or discount zone
- Range retest level

### Bullish Entry Zone

For a buy idea, the entry zone is usually below current price after bullish displacement.

Good signs:

- Price pulls back calmly.
- Pullback does not break invalidation.
- Buyers react inside the zone.
- Candle closes bullish from the zone.

Bad signs:

- Price slices through the zone.
- Candle closes below invalidation.
- Pullback is too aggressive.
- Reversal risk becomes high.

### Bearish Entry Zone

For a sell idea, the entry zone is usually above current price after bearish displacement.

Good signs:

- Price pulls back calmly.
- Pullback does not break invalidation.
- Sellers react inside the zone.
- Candle closes bearish from the zone.

Bad signs:

- Price slices through the zone.
- Candle closes above invalidation.
- Pullback is too aggressive.
- Reversal risk becomes high.

## 8. Confirmation Candle

A confirmation candle is the closed candle that turns a setup into a signal.

No unclosed candle should be trusted.

### Buy Confirmation Candle

A buy confirmation candle should show:

- Closed bullish
- Close near high
- Reaction from entry zone
- Break of minor structure, or clear rejection from the zone
- Reversal risk not high

Meaning:

Buyers accepted the entry zone.

### Sell Confirmation Candle

A sell confirmation candle should show:

- Closed bearish
- Close near low
- Reaction from entry zone
- Break of minor structure, or clear rejection from the zone
- Reversal risk not high

Meaning:

Sellers accepted the entry zone.

## 9. Pending Confirmation

A setup becomes pending when price has triggered a trade idea but a valid confirmation candle has not appeared yet.

Trader meaning:

The setup is interesting, but not ready.

Action:

Wait.

No trade should be taken yet.

### Pending Buy

The market has a bullish idea, but needs a closed bullish confirmation.

Common reason:

- Price reached the zone but has not shown enough buyer response.

### Pending Sell

The market has a bearish idea, but needs a closed bearish confirmation.

Common reason:

- Price reached the zone but has not shown enough seller response.

## 10. Expired Confirmation

An expired confirmation means price did not confirm fast enough.

Trader meaning:

The setup lost timing.

Why it matters:

Good trades usually respond quickly after reaching the entry zone.

If price drifts too long:

- Momentum may be gone.
- Liquidity condition may no longer matter.
- RR may worsen.
- The setup may become stale.

## 11. Invalidated Setup

Invalidation means the trade idea is no longer valid.

### Bullish Invalidation

A bullish setup is invalidated when price closes below the bullish invalidation level.

Common invalidation levels:

- Sweep low
- Pullback low
- Setup zone low
- Structural low

Trader meaning:

Buyers failed to defend the area.

### Bearish Invalidation

A bearish setup is invalidated when price closes above the bearish invalidation level.

Common invalidation levels:

- Sweep high
- Pullback high
- Setup zone high
- Structural high

Trader meaning:

Sellers failed to defend the area.

## 12. Stop Loss Level

Stop loss is placed where the trade idea becomes wrong.

It should not be random.

### Buy Stop Loss

For a buy, stop loss is usually below:

- Sweep low
- Pullback low
- Setup zone low
- Confirmation candle low
- Recent structural low

Good buy stop:

- Below logical invalidation
- Not too tight
- Not too wide
- Allows normal market noise

Bad buy stop:

- Inside the entry zone
- Above invalidation
- Too far away for the target
- Too tight in volatile conditions

### Sell Stop Loss

For a sell, stop loss is usually above:

- Sweep high
- Pullback high
- Setup zone high
- Confirmation candle high
- Recent structural high

Good sell stop:

- Above logical invalidation
- Not too tight
- Not too wide
- Allows normal market noise

Bad sell stop:

- Inside the entry zone
- Below invalidation
- Too far away for the target
- Too tight in volatile conditions

## 13. Take Profit Level

Take profit is usually placed at the next logical liquidity area.

### Buy Targets

Buy targets are usually above entry:

- Previous swing high
- Buy-side liquidity
- Equal highs
- Range high
- Session high
- Unmitigated resistance area

Good buy target:

- Above entry
- Clear liquidity destination
- Enough room for required RR

### Sell Targets

Sell targets are usually below entry:

- Previous swing low
- Sell-side liquidity
- Equal lows
- Range low
- Session low
- Unmitigated support area

Good sell target:

- Below entry
- Clear liquidity destination
- Enough room for required RR

## 14. Risk-Reward

Risk-reward decides whether the trade is worth taking.

### Calibration Level

Minimum RR:

```text
1.0R
```

Trader meaning:

Used for research and broad signal discovery.

### Easy Scalping Level

Minimum RR:

```text
1.2R
```

Trader meaning:

Shorter-term, more flexible, but still requires reward greater than risk.

### Normal Scalping Level

Minimum RR:

```text
1.5R
```

Trader meaning:

Balanced medium-level trade.

This is the practical level for cleaner day-trade signals.

### Pro Trader Level

Minimum RR:

```text
2.0R
```

Trader meaning:

Strict trade selection.

Needs cleaner structure, better alignment, and stronger target room.

## 15. Signal Quality Score

The score is a quality filter.

It does not replace price action.

It summarizes:

- Setup quality
- Confirmation candle quality
- Stop loss quality
- Target quality
- Context quality
- Session quality
- Volatility quality
- Reversal risk

### Setup Score

Setup score measures the quality of the idea before entry.

It considers:

- Liquidity quality
- Sweep quality
- Displacement
- Structure shift
- Location
- Session
- Volatility
- Candle psychology

### Final Signal Score

Final signal score measures whether the setup is good enough to become a trade.

It adds:

- Confirmation candle quality
- SL quality
- TP quality
- RR quality
- Context quality
- Reversal risk quality

## 16. Trading Modes

### Calibration

Purpose:

Research mode.

Behavior:

- Finds many possible signals.
- Allows weaker context.
- Allows lower RR.
- Useful for debugging and studying the market.

Trader warning:

Do not treat every calibration signal as a trade.

### Easy Scalping

Purpose:

Loose scalping mode.

Behavior:

- Requires better quality than calibration.
- Still flexible.
- Can reject if market is too choppy or conditions are poor.

Trader warning:

Best used for fast review, not high-confidence filtering.

### Normal Scalping

Purpose:

Medium-level trader mode.

Behavior:

- Requires decent setup quality.
- Requires strong enough closed confirmation.
- Requires 1.5R or better.
- Allows neutral context.
- Allows imperfect but not broken conditions.
- Keeps high reversal risk as a serious blocker.

Current normal requirements:

```text
Setup score: 55+
Final signal score: 60+
Minimum RR: 1.5R
Confirmation window: 2 closed candles
```

Trader meaning:

This mode should create fewer signals than calibration but more practical signals than pro mode.

### Pro Trader

Purpose:

Strict high-quality mode.

Behavior:

- Requires strong structure.
- Requires strong HTF alignment.
- Requires better session quality.
- Requires higher RR.
- Rejects many imperfect conditions.

Trader meaning:

This mode is selective by design.

Zero signals can be acceptable in pro mode.

## 18. Complete Buy Signal Breakdown

A buy signal forms like this:

1. Price identifies sell-side liquidity below.
2. Price sweeps below that liquidity.
3. Sellers get trapped.
4. Price rejects the low.
5. Bullish displacement appears.
6. Minor bearish structure breaks.
7. Price returns to a bullish entry zone.
8. A closed bullish candle confirms.
9. Stop loss is placed below invalidation.
10. Target is placed at buy-side liquidity.
11. RR is checked.
12. Final score is checked.
13. If all conditions pass, a buy signal appears.

## 19. Complete Sell Signal Breakdown

A sell signal forms like this:

1. Price identifies buy-side liquidity above.
2. Price sweeps above that liquidity.
3. Buyers get trapped.
4. Price rejects the high.
5. Bearish displacement appears.
6. Minor bullish structure breaks.
7. Price returns to a bearish entry zone.
8. A closed bearish candle confirms.
9. Stop loss is placed above invalidation.
10. Target is placed at sell-side liquidity.
11. RR is checked.
12. Final score is checked.
13. If all conditions pass, a sell signal appears.

## 20. Why A Signal Is Rejected

Common trader reasons:

- No valid confirmation candle
- Confirmation came too late
- Price invalidated before confirmation
- Reversal risk is high
- Stop loss is not logical
- Target does not provide enough RR
- Price is too extended
- Candle is weak or indecisive
- HTF strongly opposes direction
- Setup score is too low
- Final signal score is too low

## 21. How To Read The Signal

When a signal appears, read it in this order:

1. Direction
2. Entry price
3. Stop loss
4. Take profit
5. RR
6. Signal score
7. Setup type
8. Session
9. Warnings
10. No-repaint status

If RR is good but warnings are heavy, treat the trade as lower confidence.

If score is high but RR is weak, the trade should not be considered strong.

If confirmation is clean but context is strongly opposite, the trade is more aggressive.
